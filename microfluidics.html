Microfluidic Topology Optimization

Type Research Project
Role Lead Researcher
Duration 6 months
Status Published
View on GitHub → See Results ↓

Overview

A computational framework for optimizing microfluidic device geometries to achieve desired flow characteristics, mixing efficiency, and particle manipulation. This project uses phi.flow, a differentiable physics simulation library, to enable gradient-based optimization of complex fluid flow patterns in microscale devices.

The tool addresses critical challenges in lab-on-chip design, where traditional trial-and-error approaches are costly and time-consuming. By automating the design process, we can explore non-intuitive geometries that outperform conventional designs by significant margins.

Technical Implementation

Python phi.flow TensorFlow JAX NumPy OpenFOAM

Differentiable Physics

Leverages automatic differentiation through fluid simulations to compute exact gradients for optimization, enabling efficient convergence.

Multi-Phase Flow

Handles complex scenarios including droplet generation, mixing of multiple fluids, and particle separation with surface tension effects.

Real-Time Visualization

Animated flow visualization during optimization shows velocity fields, pressure distributions, and concentration gradients.

Dataset Generation

Automatically generates training data for ML models with paired geometry-performance samples for surrogate modeling.

Key Capabilities

  • Mixing optimization - Designs serpentine channels and chaotic advection structures for enhanced mixing
  • Flow focusing - Creates geometries for precise droplet generation and size control
  • Particle sorting - Optimizes inertial focusing and Dean flow devices for size-based separation
  • Reaction chambers - Designs residence time distributions for chemical reactions
  • Heat transfer - Optimizes cooling channels for thermal management in microreactors
  • Manufacturing constraints - Incorporates minimum feature sizes and aspect ratios for fabrication

Results & Performance

95% Mixing Efficiency

Achieved complete mixing in 3x shorter channel length compared to conventional designs

Particle Focusing

99.2% focusing efficiency for 10μm particles at 1mL/min flow rate

Fast Convergence

10x faster design optimization compared to genetic algorithms

The optimized designs have been validated through both high-fidelity CFD simulations and experimental testing. Several designs are now being used in commercial microfluidic products for point-of-care diagnostics and single-cell analysis applications.

Applications

This optimization framework has been successfully applied to various microfluidic applications:

  • Medical diagnostics - Optimized mixing chambers for rapid immunoassays
  • Drug discovery - High-throughput screening devices with controlled concentration gradients
  • Cell sorting - Inertial microfluidics for label-free cell separation
  • Chemical synthesis - Microreactors with optimized heat and mass transfer
  • Environmental monitoring - Sensors with enhanced analyte capture efficiency

Dataset & Resources

The project includes a comprehensive dataset of 10,000+ optimized microfluidic designs with their corresponding performance metrics. This dataset enables training of surrogate models for real-time design exploration and has been used by several research groups for benchmarking new optimization algorithms.

All code, datasets, and documentation are available open-source on GitHub, along with tutorials for getting started with microfluidic topology optimization.
