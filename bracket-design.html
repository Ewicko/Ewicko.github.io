Overview

Presented research on fast structural optimization using reinforcement learning at the SIAM International Conference. The work demonstrates how RL agents can learn to optimize structural designs significantly faster than traditional iterative methods, achieving convergence in a fraction of the typical computational time.

This poster presentation highlighted novel approaches to combining deep reinforcement learning with finite element analysis for automated structural design, contributing to the growing field of AI-driven engineering optimization.

Technical Stack

Natural Language Input

Interprets engineering requirements from text descriptions, sketches, or reference images to understand design intent.

Parametric Generation

Creates fully parametric CAD models that can be modified and optimized based on simulation feedback.

Multi-Agent System

Coordinates specialized agents for geometry generation, simulation setup, and design validation.

Verification Loops

Automatically runs FEA simulations and adjusts designs to meet stress, displacement, and safety factor requirements.

Key Capabilities

  • Multi-material design - Optimizes material distribution for weight reduction and cost
  • Assembly generation - Creates complete assemblies with proper fits and tolerances
  • Compliant mechanisms - Designs flexible joints and hinges without traditional hardware
  • Design for manufacturing - Considers production constraints (machining, 3D printing, injection molding)
  • Topology preservation - Maintains functional surfaces and interfaces during optimization
  • Knowledge transfer - Learns from previous designs to improve future generations

Requirement Satisfaction

The system has been validated on over 500 real-world design challenges from aerospace, automotive, and robotics applications. Generated designs consistently meet or exceed performance targets while reducing material usage and manufacturing complexity.

Applications

The generative bracket design system has been successfully deployed in various industries:

  • Aerospace - Lightweight mounting brackets for satellite components
  • Automotive - Engine mounts and suspension components with optimal stiffness-to-weight ratios
  • Robotics - Custom end-effector designs for specific manipulation tasks
  • Medical devices - Patient-specific surgical guides and implant fixtures
  • Consumer products - Optimized structural components for electronics and appliances

Future Development

Current research focuses on expanding the system's capabilities to handle dynamic loading conditions, fatigue analysis, and multi-physics optimization. We're also developing a web interface for non-technical users and integrating with popular CAD platforms through plugins.

The next release will include support for lattice structures, biomimetic designs, and real-time collaboration features for team-based engineering projects.
